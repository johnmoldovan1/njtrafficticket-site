Before you pay a New Jersey speeding ticket, check the points.

Speeding can be 2, 4, or 5 points depending on how far over the limit the ticket claims you were driving. The fine is often not the real problem. Points, insurance, license history, CDL status, and speed range can matter more.

NJ speeding ticket points

New Jersey speeding tickets commonly carry 2 points for 1-14 mph over, 4 points for 15-29 mph over, and 5 points for 30 mph or more over. Safe corridor, construction zone, and 65 mph area tickets can carry higher payable amounts.

Should you pay online?

Paying online may be treated as pleading guilty. That can add points and create a conviction before you know whether a lower-risk resolution is available. Get advice first if you have prior points, a CDL, a probationary license, an out-of-state license, an accident, or a speed 15+ mph over the limit.

Common questions about nj speeding tickets.

How many points is a NJ speeding ticket?

It depends on the speed range. Commonly, 1-14 mph over is 2 points, 15-29 mph over is 4 points, and 30+ mph over is 5 points.

Should I pay a NJ speeding ticket online?

Not until you understand the points, insurance, license, and employment consequences. Online payment can operate as a guilty plea.
